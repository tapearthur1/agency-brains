## KEY_INSIGHTS:

**1. Homepage Dominance — Healthy but Risky**
The homepage captures approximately **74% of all user traffic** (113 of ~153 unique users across the site). While strong homepage traffic is normal, this level of concentration suggests users may not be finding deeper service and location pages organically. The site needs stronger internal linking and SEO development across secondary pages.

**2. Contact Page Conversion Signal is Encouraging**
With 21 users reaching /contact-us/, the implied homepage-to-contact rate is approximately **18.6%**. This is a positive signal indicating homepage visitors have genuine intent. The priority should be protecting and improving this conversion pathway.

**3. Location Pages Are Working — But Unevenly**
Cherry Hill Township (13 users) and Mount Laurel (6 users) are drawing traffic, but **Moorestown (5 users) and Gloucester Township (2 users)** are significantly underperforming despite being key service markets. Haddonfield has **no dedicated location page appearing in this data at all**, which is a critical gap given it is one of three primary markets.

**4. Multiple 404 Errors Are Damaging SEO and UX**
At least **6 distinct 404 "Page Not Found" errors** are logged this week, including:
- /living-well-with-parkinsons/ — likely a former or broken blog post
- /booking — broken booking pathway
- /creating-a-home-care-oasis-trust-and-comfort-for-elderly-couples/ — broken blog URL
- /respite-care-giving-family-caregivers-the-break-they-deserve/ — broken blog URL
- /locations/cherry-hill-township/ and /locations/gloucester-township/ — wrong URL structure being indexed

These 404s are wasting crawl budget, creating dead ends for real visitors, and likely hurting rankings for important keywords like **Parkinson's care** and **respite care**.

**5. Blog Traffic is Critically Underperforming**
The /blog/ index page received only **3 users**. Most individual blog posts received **zero traffic**. Given the volume of content published (posts on Parkinson's, post-hospital recovery, elderly home care, respite care), this suggests either poor keyword targeting, insufficient internal linking to blog content, or indexing/technical issues.

**6. Key Pages Are Receiving Zero Traffic**
High-value pages including **/faq/**, **/paying-for-home-care/** (only 2 users), **/post-hospital-recovery-support/**, and several blog posts are receiving no measurable traffic. These pages have strong SEO potential that is currently unrealized.

**7. Careers Page Has Surprising Traction**
/our-team/ (Careers) received 5 users — comparable to the Moorestown location page. This suggests some organic interest in employment opportunities, which could be worth a dedicated SEO effort to attract caregiving staff.

**Priority 3 — Rebuild and Republish Key Blog Posts**
Recreate the following as properly optimized, standalone posts with correct URLs:
- **Parkinson's Care at Home** — target "Parkinson's home care NJ," "in-home Parkinson's care Cherry Hill"

These topics have demonstrated search demand and the content already existed, making this a recovery rather than creation task.

**Priority 5 — Revitalize Zero-Traffic Blog Posts**
